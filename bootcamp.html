Week 1

Research & Scope

Start with what excites you. A problem you’ve noticed, an industry you’re curious about, a frustration you’ve lived with. This week is about turning raw curiosity into a real plan.

  • Market research. Who has this problem? How big is the opportunity? What solutions exist today and where do they fall short?
  • Business model. How does this make money? Who pays, how much, and why? Revenue streams, cost structure, unit economics.
  • Value proposition. Why would someone choose your product over the alternative — including doing nothing?
  • Executive summary. One page that captures the problem, the solution, the market, and the plan. If you can’t explain it clearly, you don’t understand it yet.
  • Scoping session with mentors. What’s realistic in 4 weeks of building? What features are core vs. nice-to-have? Where are the technical risks?

By the end of Week 1, every team has a one-page brief, a scoped feature set, and a clear build plan for the next four weeks.

Weeks 2–5

Build

This is where the bootcamp lives. Every week is a complete cycle of the Lean Startup loop: build something, ship it, measure how real people use it, learn from the data, then decide what to do next. Four full rotations in four weeks.

  • Week 2: Build your MVP — the smallest thing that tests your riskiest assumption. Ship it to your cohort. Measure: did they use it the way you expected? Learn: what was wrong about your hypothesis?
  • Week 3: Based on what you learned, pivot or persevere. Build the next iteration. Ship again to a wider group. Measure real usage data — not what people say, what they do. Learn what actually matters to users.
  • Week 4: Another full cycle. The product should be unrecognizable from Week 2. Every decision is now backed by data, not gut feeling. Ship, measure, learn.
  • Week 5: Final cycle. Polish what the data tells you matters. Cut what it tells you doesn’t. Get it to a state you’d be proud to demo live.

Daily standups. Weekly demos. Mentors on-call for technical architecture, product strategy, and pivot-or-persevere decisions throughout.

Weeks 6–7

Launch & Learn

Your product goes to real users outside the cohort. This is where you learn what the market actually thinks — not what your teammates politely suggested.

  • Real user acquisition. Get your product in front of people who didn’t build it. Track signups, engagement, and retention.
  • Metrics that matter. Define your north star metric. Instrument your product. Learn to read what the data is telling you.
  • Cross-team beta testing. Other bootcamp teams run structured testing on your product and deliver honest, detailed feedback.
  • Iterate fast. Two weeks of rapid improvement based on real usage data, not guesses.
Week 8

Demo Day

The finish line. Every team gets 10 minutes to pitch and demo their product live to a panel of mentors, industry guests, and investors.

  • The pitch. Problem, solution, market, traction, business model — delivered with clarity and conviction.
  • Live demo. No slides. No mockups. A real product, running live, used by real people.
  • Q&A. Defend your decisions. Show you understand your market. Prove you can think on your feet.

You leave the bootcamp with a working product, a pitch deck, real user data, and the skills to do it again.
